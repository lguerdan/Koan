Beyond the Band

Nearly $23 billion dollars are spent treating anxiety and panic disorders in the United States of America each year treating physical symptoms of distress without tackling the root of the problem - underlying cognitive state. Koan is a project to empower people to discover more about the triggers and side-effects of panic attacks. Our mobile SMS intervention is a first step in identifying a panic attack. 

These strategiess may also help: 
         1. Acknowledge the panic attack. 

         2. Attempt to make self more comfortable (listen to music, count breaths, etc.)

         3. Continue doing what you were doing before as best as you can.

         4. Contact a trusted family member or friend who understands your condition.

Research

The original inspiration for the Koan project came from this publication on methods of stress detection using electroencephalogram (EEG) data. Because these waves represent differing levels of mental activity, their relative frequency can be used to detect distress such as panic attacks.
Once data is collected from the Muse and processed, we utilize frequency band variability and relative signal strengths to detect changes in mental activity. This approach was influenced by work on microstates and classification algorithmns. Once we detect abnormal variations in EEG signal, especially changes in gamma wave frequency, we reach out to the user to learn more and intervene if neccessary.
